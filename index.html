Welcome

A Place of Stillness in a Noisy World

St Paul's is a parish church at the heart of Hasland, offering a space of stillness in a noisy world and a place of depth in an often distracted culture. We are a welcoming church in Chesterfield, Derbyshire — centred on prayer, worship, and community.

Here you will find worship that is reverent and prayerful, rooted in Scripture and shaped by the historic Christian faith. Our life together is centred on the Holy Eucharist — a service of Word and Sacrament that has sustained Christians for centuries.

"How beautiful are the feet of those who bring good news!"
— Romans 10:15

If you are spiritually curious, returning to faith, carrying questions, or simply longing for something deeper, you are warmly welcome. You do not need to have everything figured out. You can come as you are.

Your First Visit

Planning Your First Visit?

Join us this Sunday
Sung Parish Mass — 10.30am

A reverent service with hymns, prayer, Scripture, and Holy Communion. Arrive 10 minutes early to give yourself time to orientate, then take a seat anywhere. Service books are provided, and you can simply follow along.

If you would like a quiet word before the service, someone will be glad to help. You are welcome to attend whether you wish to receive Holy Communion or not. Many people come simply to pray, reflect, and be present.

For Visitors

What to Expect

✦ Traditional Anglican liturgy in a catholic style
✦ Vestments, candles, and ceremonial reflecting the Church's historic worship
✦ A clear structure that enables both regular worshippers and visitors to participate
✦ A sermon rooted in Scripture and the Church's teaching
✦ Holy Communion offered to all baptised Christians

If you are new or unsure about anything during the service, simply follow those around you — or feel free to speak to a member of the welcome team beforehand.

Special Services

Holy Week & Easter at St Paul's

Holy Week is the most important week in the Christian year. During these days we remember the final events of Jesus' life — from His welcome into Jerusalem, through His death on the cross, to the joy of His resurrection on Easter Day.

Palm Sunday Sunday 13th April – 10.30am

Sung Mass with Blessing of Palms and Procession from the Lychgate. We begin outside the church with the blessing of palm crosses before entering in procession.

Our Faith

What We Believe

We believe in one God — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — as revealed in Holy Scripture and summarised in the ancient Creeds of the Church.

As an Anglo-Catholic parish, we hold to the fullness of the Catholic faith as received by the Church of England. We believe that the Church is called to be a sign of God's kingdom on earth.

Holy Eucharist

The central act of Christian worship, the source and summit of our life together.

Baptism

The sacrament of new birth, by which we are made members of Christ's Body.

Holy Scripture

The Word of God containing all things necessary for salvation.
